# Education / EdTech — User Acquisition Analysis

You are evaluating the **user acquisition** of an education/edtech idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Determine the primary acquisition motion: B2C viral, B2B sales, or B2B2C**. EdTech acquisition varies dramatically by model. Consumer products need viral loops or content marketing. Institutional products need sales teams and procurement navigation. B2B2C (sell to institution, users are students/employees) combines both. Identify which motion fits and assess feasibility.

2. **Assess the motivation and retention challenge**. Education has a unique acquisition problem: users are motivated at signup but motivation decays rapidly. Completion rates for online courses average 5-15%. The acquisition strategy must account for not just getting users but keeping them engaged. Evaluate natural retention drivers.

3. **Check for built-in distribution advantages**. The best edtech products have distribution built into the product: teachers assign it (institutional mandate), employers require it (compliance training), or learners share it (study groups). Evaluate whether the idea has a natural distribution mechanism or requires pure marketing spend to grow.

## Domain Knowledge

### Acquisition Channels by Model

#### Consumer (B2C)

| Channel | Effectiveness | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| App Store Optimization (ASO) | High for mobile apps | Low ($5K-15K) | 3-6 months |
| Content/SEO | High (educational content ranks well) | Medium ($10K-30K/mo) | 6-12 months |
| Social media (TikTok, YouTube) | High for engaging content | Low-Medium | 1-6 months |
| Paid social (Meta, TikTok) | Medium (high intent variability) | $5-30 CAC | Immediate |
| Referral/word-of-mouth | High (if product is good) | Low | 3-12 months |
| PR/media coverage | High for novel products | Medium ($5-15K/mo agency) | Variable |
| Freemium funnel | High (Duolingo model) | Product investment | 6-12 months |

#### Institutional (B2B)

| Channel | Effectiveness | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct sales (schools/districts) | Essential for K-12 | $50K-200K/yr per rep | 6-18 months to first deals |
| Conference/trade shows | High for relationship building | $5K-50K per event | Seasonal |
| Pilot programs | Essential for proving value | Product + support cost | 1-3 months per pilot |
| Teacher/professor champions | Critical for bottom-up adoption | Community investment | 6-12 months |
| RFP responses | Required for large districts | Sales team time | Variable |
| Partner channel (resellers) | Scales institutional reach | Revenue share | 3-6 months to establish |

#### B2B2C (Enterprise → Employee)

| Channel | Effectiveness | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| L&D department sales | Primary channel | $100K-300K/yr per rep | 1-6 months |
| HR/benefits integration | High for compliance training | Integration investment | 3-6 months |
| Platform partnerships | Scalable (Slack, Teams integrations) | Development time | 3-6 months |
| Content marketing (LinkedIn) | Good for thought leadership | $5-15K/mo | 3-6 months |

### Teacher as Gateway Strategy

- Teachers are the most powerful distribution channel in K-12 edtech
- Strategy: free tool for teachers → teachers assign to students → school sees usage → institutional purchase
- Kahoot!, ClassDojo, and Google Classroom all grew this way
- Critical: the free tool must deliver genuine value to teachers, not just be a trial-limited version
- Average teacher influences 25-150 students per year

### The Motivation Decay Problem

| Metric | Industry Average | Top Performers |
|---|---|---|
| MOOC completion rate | 5-15% | 30-40% (cohort-based) |
| App Day-1 retention | 25-35% | 50%+ (Duolingo) |
| App Day-30 retention | 5-10% | 20-30% (Duolingo) |
| Free-to-paid conversion | 2-5% | 8-10% (Duolingo) |
| Annual subscription renewal | 40-60% | 70%+ (enterprise) |

### Retention Drivers That Improve Acquisition ROI

- **Streaks and gamification**: Duolingo's streak mechanic drives daily engagement (65%+ of DAU maintain a streak)
- **Cohort/social learning**: peer accountability improves completion 3-5x vs self-paced
- **Institutional mandate**: employer or school requires completion (100% "acquisition" within the institution)
- **Credential motivation**: if completion leads to a recognized credential, motivation persists
- **Progress visibility**: showing tangible skill progress retains better than content consumption metrics

### CAC Benchmarks

| Segment | Typical CAC | LTV:CAC Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer freemium | $1-10 (organic/ASO) | 3:1+ | Volume-dependent |
| Consumer paid | $15-50 | 3:1+ | High churn erodes LTV |
| K-12 institutional | $5-20/student | 5:1+ | Long sales cycle |
| Higher-ed | $50-200/student | 3:1+ | Procurement complexity |
| Enterprise L&D | $200-2,000/seat | 4:1+ | Annual contracts help |

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: Built-in distribution mechanism — institutional mandate, teacher-as-gateway with proven adoption, or strong viral/social loop. Multiple scalable channels. Low CAC with high retention. Example: an AI tool that teachers adopt for free, assign to students, and schools purchase district-wide licenses.

- **60-79**: Clear channel strategy with 2-3 viable paths. Reasonable CAC for the revenue model. Some organic/viral potential. Example: a professional certification platform with SEO for target keywords, LinkedIn content marketing, and employer partnerships.

- **40-59**: Acquisition possible but requires significant investment. Long institutional sales cycles or expensive paid acquisition. Retention challenges may undermine acquisition ROI. Example: a consumer learning app requiring paid social to acquire users who churn after 2-3 months.

- **20-39**: High CAC, narrow channels, or motivation/retention challenges that make acquisition ROI negative. Long sales cycles with uncertain conversion. Example: a niche edtech product for a small institutional market with 12+ month sales cycles and no organic growth path.

- **0-19**: No clear path to users. Product requires behavior change that users resist. No institutional buyer or mandate. Cold market with no demand signals. Example: a voluntary professional development tool in a field with no continuing education requirements and free alternatives.
