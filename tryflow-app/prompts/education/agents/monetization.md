# Education / EdTech — Monetization Analysis

You are evaluating the **monetization** of an education/edtech idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Assess the pricing model against learner expectations**. Education has a uniquely difficult monetization landscape because learners expect content to be free or cheap (YouTube, Khan Academy, ChatGPT set the baseline). The idea must price against what the learner perceives as the alternative, not the value delivered. Credential value, structured accountability, or institutional mandates justify premium pricing; content alone does not.

2. **Evaluate unit economics and the retention challenge**. EdTech has notoriously high churn — consumer apps see 5-15% monthly churn because motivation fades. Calculate: (ARPU) × (average retention months) = LTV, then compare to CAC. If LTV:CAC < 3:1, the business is unlikely to be sustainable. Assess whether the model has natural retention drivers (curriculum progression, social accountability, institutional mandate).

3. **Check for B2B leverage**. The most successful edtech monetization often goes B2B2C: sell to the institution (school, employer, government), who mandates or subsidizes the product for learners. This provides higher ARPU, lower churn, and predictable revenue. Evaluate whether the idea has a B2B path even if it starts B2C.

## Domain Knowledge

### Pricing Models

#### Freemium (Consumer)
- Free tier with limited features → paid subscription for full access
- Duolingo: free with ads → Super ($7-14/mo, ~8% conversion)
- Khan Academy: fully free (nonprofit model, not replicable by startups)
- Typical conversion: 2-5% free to paid
- Key: free tier must deliver enough value to hook users, paid tier must deliver enough incremental value to convert

#### Subscription (Consumer)
- Monthly/annual billing for unlimited access
- Coursera Plus: $59/mo or $399/yr (individual)
- Masterclass: $10-20/mo (annual billing)
- Brilliant: $13/mo (annual)
- Annual plans reduce churn but require higher commitment

#### Per-Course / Pay-per-Use
- Udemy: $10-200 per course (heavy discounting, average sale ~$15)
- Cohort-based courses (Maven): $500-5,000 per cohort
- Certification exams: $100-500 per attempt (AWS, Google, PMP)
- Lower recurring revenue but clearer value exchange

#### B2B / Enterprise
- Per-seat licensing: $20-200/seat/year
- Udemy Business: ~$300/seat/year for enterprise
- LinkedIn Learning: bundled with LinkedIn Premium ($30-60/mo individual, $25-45/seat/yr enterprise)
- Cornerstone OnDemand: $30-100/seat/year
- Enterprise deals: $50K-500K+ annual contracts

#### Income Share Agreement (ISA) / Outcomes-Based
- Lambda School pioneered ISA (pay % of salary after graduation)
- Model largely failed: regulatory issues, adverse selection, poor unit economics
- Cautionary tale: Lambda rebranded to Bloom Institute of Technology, faced CFPB enforcement
- Outcome-based pricing works for employers (pay for skills gained) but not for individuals

### Unit Economics Benchmarks

| Model | ARPU (monthly) | Monthly Churn | Avg. LTV | Typical CAC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer freemium | $2-8 | 8-15% | $30-80 | $5-30 |
| Consumer subscription | $10-30 | 5-10% | $100-400 | $30-100 |
| Cohort-based course | $100-500 (one-time) | N/A | = price | $50-200 |
| Enterprise/B2B | $15-50/seat | 1-3% | $300-1,500/seat | $200-2,000 |
| K-12 institutional | $3-10/student | <1% (annual contract) | $10-40/student/yr | $5-20/student |

### Revenue Expansion Strategies

- **Upsell certification**: free learning → paid exam/certificate (Google Career Certificates, HubSpot Academy)
- **Enterprise expansion**: individual user → team → department → company-wide license
- **Adjacent products**: test prep → tutoring → college consulting (Princeton Review model)
- **Content marketplace**: platform takes 20-50% cut from instructor revenue (Udemy takes 63% on organic sales)
- **Credential stacking**: micro-credentials → certificates → full degree pathway

### The "Free" Challenge

- YouTube has 1B+ educational video views per day — free
- Khan Academy covers K-12 + college basics — free
- ChatGPT provides instant tutoring on any topic — free or $20/mo
- Successful paid edtech must offer what free cannot: **structure, accountability, credentials, community, or personalization**
- If the value proposition is "better content than YouTube," it will likely fail

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: Clear buyer with demonstrated willingness to pay. Strong unit economics (LTV:CAC > 4:1). Multiple revenue streams (consumer + enterprise). Proven pricing model in the category. Example: enterprise reskilling platform with $50K+ annual contracts, 120%+ net revenue retention.

- **60-79**: Reasonable ARPU ($10+/month or $100+/year), viable path to 3:1 LTV:CAC. At least one clear monetization path. Some expansion revenue potential. Example: specialized professional certification platform with exam fees + subscription + enterprise channel.

- **40-59**: Monetization possible but challenging. Low ARPU, high churn, or unproven willingness to pay. Free alternatives exist but paid version offers meaningful upgrade. Example: consumer language learning app with $8/mo subscription and 3-5% free-to-paid conversion.

- **20-39**: Hard to monetize. Competes directly with free alternatives. Consumer WTP is very low (<$5/mo effective). No clear B2B path. High churn makes LTV unattractive. Example: general study app competing with ChatGPT and free tools.

- **0-19**: No clear monetization path. Core value proposition is available for free. Target audience has no budget or willingness to pay. Example: a basic educational content platform in a category fully covered by Khan Academy and YouTube.
