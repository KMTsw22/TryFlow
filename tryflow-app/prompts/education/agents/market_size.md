# Education / EdTech — Market Size Analysis

You are evaluating the **market size** of an education/edtech idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Identify the target segment and buyer**. The global education market exceeds $7T, but edtech captures a fraction of that. More importantly, willingness to pay varies enormously: governments and enterprises spend freely on proven solutions, while individual consumers resist paying for education (especially when free alternatives exist). Determine who pays — student (B2C), school/university (B2B), employer (B2B2C), or government — and size the market accordingly.

2. **Estimate per-user revenue and realistic penetration**. B2C edtech has notoriously low ARPU: Duolingo averages ~$5/month across all users (including free), Coursera Plus is $59/month. Enterprise training commands $20-100+/seat/year. K-12 institutional pricing ranges from $5-50/student/year. Map the idea to the correct revenue range and estimate a realistic user base.

3. **Assess the "free" problem**. Education has a unique challenge: much of the best content is free (Khan Academy, YouTube, MIT OpenCourseWare, ChatGPT). Willingness to pay depends on one of: (a) credential/certification value, (b) structured accountability, (c) personalized experience, or (d) institutional mandate. If the idea competes directly with free content without adding one of these, the addressable market is much smaller than it appears.

### Geographic Considerations

- **US**: largest edtech market (~$80B+). High institutional spend, mature market.
- **India**: 2nd largest (~$10B edtech, rapidly growing). Price-sensitive but massive volume. Test prep (JEE, NEET) is enormous. Post-Byju's correction, investors cautious.
- **China**: heavy government regulation of edtech (2021 crackdown on private tutoring). Risky market but massive demand.
- **Southeast Asia**: fast-growing, mobile-first, price-sensitive. Ruangguru (Indonesia), Topica (Vietnam).
- **South Korea**: ~$20B+ private education market (hagwon culture). Highest per-capita education spend globally. Strong demand for English, coding, test prep.
- **Europe**: fragmented by language/regulation. Strong in corporate training.

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: TAM exceeds $10B in a fast-growing segment (>15% CAGR). Clear buyer with demonstrated willingness to pay and budget line item. Multiple revenue streams (consumer + enterprise). Example: AI-powered corporate reskilling platform in a segment where companies actively budget for training.

- **60-79**: TAM of $1-10B with solid growth. At least one proven buyer channel. Per-user economics align with comparable companies. Example: specialized coding education platform for enterprise developers with clear L&D budget allocation.

- **40-59**: TAM of $500M-$1B or large market where free alternatives capture most demand. Willingness to pay exists but is price-sensitive. Example: a consumer language learning app for a less-common language pair with moderate global demand.

- **20-39**: Small addressable market (<$500M) or heavy reliance on consumer willingness to pay in a category where free alternatives dominate. Comparable companies have struggled with monetization. Example: a general-purpose study tool competing with free options like Quizlet, Anki, ChatGPT.

- **0-19**: Tiny market or fundamentally free category. No clear buyer or budget. Value proposition does not justify payment when free alternatives exist. Example: a basic video lecture platform competing with YouTube and free university courses.
