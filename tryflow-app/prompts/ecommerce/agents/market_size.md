# E-Commerce / D2C — Market Size Analysis

You are evaluating the **market size** of an e-commerce/D2C idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Identify the product category and its online penetration**. Global e-commerce exceeds $6T, but penetration varies wildly by category. Electronics and apparel are 30-50% online, while grocery is still ~12% and furniture ~20%. A category with low but rising online penetration represents a tailwind; a fully penetrated category means competing for existing share.

2. **Estimate realistic revenue using AOV, repeat rate, and addressable customers**. Bottom-up: (target customers in geography) × (conversion rate) × (AOV) × (annual purchase frequency). D2C brands typically see 1-5% website conversion rates, with AOV ranging from $30 (consumables) to $500+ (furniture/electronics). Repeat purchase rate is the critical multiplier — 1x purchasers build unsustainable businesses.

3. **Assess category economics and venture scalability**. Not all large markets are venture-appropriate. A $10B market with 10% gross margins and 1x purchase frequency is worse than a $1B market with 70% margins and monthly repeat. Check if comparable companies have reached $100M+ revenue and whether the category supports venture-scale outcomes.

### Geographic Considerations

- **US**: largest single e-commerce market (~$1.1T). Mature, competitive, high CAC. D2C brands must differentiate strongly.
- **Europe**: ~$600B e-commerce. Fragmented by language/regulation but growing. Strong in fashion (Zalando, ASOS).
- **China**: ~$2.5T e-commerce (largest globally). Dominated by Alibaba (Tmall/Taobao), JD.com, Pinduoduo. Nearly impossible for foreign D2C to enter without local partner.
- **Southeast Asia**: ~$100B and growing rapidly. Shopee and Lazada dominant. Mobile-first, price-sensitive.
- **South Korea**: ~$150B e-commerce. Coupang dominant (~25% market share). Naver Shopping, SSG. High online penetration across categories.

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: Category exceeds $10B with rising online penetration (>15% CAGR). High gross margins (60%+), strong repeat purchase dynamics. Comparable D2C brands have reached $500M+ revenue. Example: premium beauty/skincare D2C in a fast-growing subcategory with subscription potential.

- **60-79**: Category of $1-10B with solid growth. Reasonable margins (40-60%) and some repeat purchase potential. Clear customer willingness to pay premium for D2C value proposition. Example: specialty food/beverage brand in a growing health-conscious subcategory.

- **40-59**: Category of $500M-$1B or large category with narrow addressable slice. Margins are moderate (30-50%) or repeat purchases are infrequent. Market is valid but venture-scale outcome is uncertain. Example: home decor brand in a specific aesthetic niche.

- **20-39**: Small market (<$500M addressable) or category with poor unit economics (low margins, 1x purchase, high return rates). Comparable companies have struggled to scale. Example: a niche hobby product with a small enthusiast community.

- **0-19**: Tiny or shrinking market. Low margins, no repeat purchase, commodity pricing. Not venture-scale. Example: a generic consumer electronics accessory competing purely on price.
