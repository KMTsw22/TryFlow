# E-Commerce / D2C — Regulation Analysis

You are evaluating the **regulatory environment** of an e-commerce/D2C idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Identify product-specific regulations**. Regulatory burden in e-commerce depends almost entirely on what is being sold. A cotton t-shirt faces minimal regulation; a dietary supplement requires FDA compliance; a children's toy needs CPSC testing. Map the idea to the correct regulatory category and assess the cost and timeline for compliance.

2. **Assess cross-border and trade regulations**. If the product is manufactured overseas (especially China), import duties, tariffs, and customs compliance apply. Section 301 tariffs on Chinese goods range from 7.5-25%. De minimis threshold ($800 in the US) affects low-cost imports. Evaluate how trade policy impacts landed cost and competitive positioning.

3. **Check for advertising and claims restrictions**. The FTC actively enforces rules on product claims, testimonials, and influencer disclosures. Health claims on supplements require specific FDA-compliant language. "Made in USA" claims have strict requirements. Environmental/sustainability claims ("eco-friendly," "biodegradable") face increasing scrutiny (greenwashing enforcement).

## Domain Knowledge

### Product Safety Regulations

| Product Type | US Regulation | EU Regulation | Cost/Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| General consumer products | CPSC compliance, ASTM standards | CE marking, General Product Safety Directive | $2K-10K, 1-3 months |
| Children's products (under 12) | CPSIA testing (lead, phthalates), ASTM F963 | EN 71 toy safety | $5K-20K, 2-4 months |
| Electronics | FCC Part 15 (EMC), UL safety | CE (RED, LVD, EMC directives) | $5K-30K, 2-6 months |
| Food/Beverage | FDA registration, nutrition labeling, FSMA | EU food safety regulations | $3K-15K, 1-6 months |
| Supplements/Vitamins | FDA (DSHEA framework, GMP compliance) | EU Novel Food Regulation | $10K-50K, 3-12 months |
| Cosmetics/Skincare | FDA (limited pre-market), state regulations | EU Cosmetics Regulation (1223/2009), CPNP notification | $5K-25K, 2-6 months |
| Textiles/Apparel | CPSC (flammability), FTC labeling rules | REACH compliance, textile labeling | $1K-5K, 1-2 months |

### Advertising & Claims

- **FTC enforcement**: endorsement guides require material connection disclosure (influencer #ad). Substantiation required for product claims. Recent FTC actions against misleading health claims, fake reviews, and undisclosed paid endorsements.
- **Health claims**: supplements cannot claim to "cure, treat, or prevent" disease without FDA approval. Structure/function claims ("supports immune health") permitted with disclaimer.
- **Environmental claims**: FTC Green Guides govern "eco-friendly," "recyclable," "biodegradable" claims. EU Green Claims Directive (2024+) requires substantiation. Greenwashing lawsuits increasing.
- **"Made in USA"**: FTC requires "all or virtually all" to be US-made. "Assembled in USA" has lower threshold but still requires significant US processing.

### Trade & Import Regulations

- **Section 301 tariffs**: 7.5-25% on most Chinese imports (product-dependent). Additional tariffs on specific categories.
- **De minimis ($800 US)**: shipments under $800 avoid duties. Temu/Shein exploit this for direct-to-consumer shipping. Under legislative scrutiny — may be reduced or eliminated.
- **Customs compliance**: accurate HS code classification, country of origin marking, import bond requirements.
- **EU IOSS**: Import One-Stop Shop for VAT on goods under €150.

### Consumer Protection

- **Return policies**: no federal US requirement but FTC requires clear disclosure. Many states have specific rules. EU: 14-day mandatory cooling-off period for online purchases.
- **Subscription billing**: FTC "click-to-cancel" rule requires easy cancellation. Auto-renewal laws vary by state. "Negative option" enforcement increasing.
- **Data privacy**: GDPR (EU), CCPA/CPRA (California), state privacy laws proliferating. E-commerce collects significant PII — compliance required for email marketing, customer data, analytics.
- **Payment processing**: PCI-DSS compliance required for handling card data (typically handled by Shopify/Stripe but still merchant responsibility).

### Regulatory Tailwinds

- Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) laws create compliance burden that favors established players over small competitors
- Stricter sustainability disclosure requirements advantage genuinely sustainable brands over greenwashers
- FDA modernization of supplement/cosmetic regulation (MoCRA 2022) raises the compliance bar, which acts as a barrier to entry for low-quality competitors

## Scoring Guide (Higher = Easier)

- **80-100**: Minimal regulation — standard consumer goods with no special compliance. Basic CPSC/CE requirements only. No restricted claims or ingredients. Example: home decor, stationery, fashion accessories.

- **60-79**: Standard compliance required — product labeling, basic safety testing, FTC advertising rules. Manageable cost ($5-15K) and timeline (1-3 months). Example: apparel, general beauty products, pet accessories.

- **40-59**: Moderate regulation — product-specific testing, certification, or registration required. $15-50K compliance cost, 3-6 months. May need ongoing compliance monitoring. Example: supplements, children's products, electronics.

- **20-39**: Heavy regulation — significant pre-market requirements, ongoing testing, or restricted ingredients/claims. $50K+ compliance cost, 6-12+ months. Limits formulation or marketing flexibility. Example: FDA-regulated food products with health claims, medical-adjacent devices.

- **0-19**: Prohibitive regulation — requires licenses, extensive clinical testing, or faces legal uncertainty that could shut down the business. Example: selling actual pharmaceuticals, controlled substances, or products banned in key markets.
