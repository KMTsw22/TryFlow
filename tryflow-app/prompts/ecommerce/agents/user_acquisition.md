# E-Commerce / D2C — User Acquisition Analysis

You are evaluating the **user acquisition** of an e-commerce/D2C idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Map the most effective acquisition channels for this category and price point**. Channel effectiveness in e-commerce varies enormously by product type. Visually appealing products (fashion, beauty, home) thrive on Instagram/TikTok. Considered purchases (mattress, electronics) need SEO/content. Repeat consumables benefit from referral and subscription loops. Match the idea to the channels where its target customers discover and buy products.

2. **Estimate blended CAC and first-order profitability**. The critical D2C question is whether you can acquire a customer profitably on the first order (rare) or whether you need repeat purchases to recoup CAC (common). Calculate: (ad spend + creative costs + landing page costs) / conversions = CAC. Compare against first-order contribution margin. If CAC > first-order margin, the business depends entirely on retention.

3. **Assess channel diversification and organic potential**. Over-reliance on a single paid channel (especially Meta) is the #1 killer of D2C brands. iOS 14.5 proved this. Evaluate whether the idea has organic acquisition potential: SEO, word-of-mouth, content virality, PR, community. Brands that achieve 40%+ organic traffic have dramatically better economics.

## Domain Knowledge

### Acquisition Channels by Effectiveness

#### 1. Paid Social (Meta, TikTok)
- **Meta (Instagram/Facebook)**: still the largest D2C acquisition channel. CPM: $8-20 (US). Average conversion rate: 1-3%. CAC: $15-80 depending on category and AOV. iOS 14.5 reduced targeting effectiveness — broad targeting + creative testing is the new playbook.
- **TikTok Ads**: lower CPM ($4-12) but lower intent. Best for impulse purchases, viral products, younger demographics. TikTok Shop combines content + commerce — 2-5x better ROAS than standard TikTok ads for some brands.
- **Creative is the new targeting**: with privacy changes, ad creative quality matters more than audience targeting. User-generated content (UGC) style ads outperform polished brand ads by 2-4x on TikTok.

#### 2. Search (Google Shopping, SEO)
- **Google Shopping/PMax**: high-intent buyers actively searching. CPC: $0.50-3.00. ROAS: 3-8x for most categories. Essential for considered purchases.
- **SEO**: long-term organic acquisition. Target product reviews, "best [category]" queries, educational content. 6-12 months to see results. Compounds over time — best long-term CAC channel.
- **Amazon SEO**: if selling on Amazon, organic ranking on Amazon search is critical (70%+ of Amazon purchases start on Amazon search).

#### 3. Influencer & Creator Marketing
- **Micro-influencers (10K-100K followers)**: $100-1,000 per post. Higher engagement, more authentic. Best for niche products.
- **Mid-tier (100K-1M)**: $1,000-10,000 per post. Good reach-to-cost ratio.
- **Macro/Celebrity (1M+)**: $10,000-500,000+ per post. Brand awareness, not direct conversion.
- **Affiliate/commission model**: pay per conversion (10-30% commission). Lower risk, aligns incentives.
- UGC creators: pay for content ($100-500 per video), use in paid ads. Not about their audience — about their content creation skills.

#### 4. Email & SMS (Retention)
- **Email**: Klaviyo is the dominant platform. Average email revenue: 20-30% of total for mature D2C brands. Flow automation (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase) drives most email revenue.
- **SMS**: 15-25% click-through rate (vs 2-5% email). Growing channel, higher engagement but higher opt-out risk. Attentive, Postscript are key platforms.
- **Retention economics**: acquiring a repeat customer costs 5-7x less than a new customer. Brands with strong email/SMS programs have 30-50% lower blended CAC.

#### 5. Marketplace Presence
- **Amazon**: 60%+ of US product searches start on Amazon. Selling on Amazon provides distribution but at 15%+ referral fee + FBA costs. Risk: Amazon owns the customer relationship.
- **Etsy**: strong for handmade, unique, gift products. Lower fees than Amazon. Smaller audience.
- **Multi-channel**: most successful D2C brands sell on own site + Amazon + 1-2 other channels.

#### 6. Organic & PR
- **Earned media**: product reviews in Wirecutter, Vogue, GQ, etc. can drive massive traffic spikes. PR agency cost: $5-15K/mo.
- **Viral moments**: unboxing videos, TikTok organic virality. Can't be planned but can be facilitated through product design and packaging.
- **Community building**: brand communities (Reddit, Discord, Facebook Groups) drive organic referrals and repeat purchase.

### Post-iOS 14.5 Reality

- Meta ad performance declined 30-40% for many D2C brands
- Attribution is harder — last-click understates Meta, overstates Google
- Broad targeting + creative testing replaced granular audience targeting
- First-party data (email, SMS lists) became critical for retargeting
- Server-side tracking (Meta CAPI) partially recovers signal
- Diversification to TikTok, Google, and organic channels is essential

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: Natural virality or organic discovery loop. Multiple scalable channels with proven economics. Low CAC ($15-30) with strong first-order or fast-repeat economics. 40%+ organic traffic achievable. Example: a visually stunning, giftable product with TikTok virality, strong SEO potential, and influencer appeal.

- **60-79**: 2-3 viable acquisition channels with reasonable CAC ($30-60). Paid acquisition is profitable with repeat purchases. Some organic potential through content or community. Example: a niche beauty brand with influencer-friendly product, SEO strategy, and growing email list.

- **40-59**: Acquisition possible but expensive ($60-100+ CAC) or concentrated in 1-2 channels. Paid acquisition requires repeat purchases to become profitable. Limited organic potential. Example: a mid-price home goods brand dependent on Meta ads with moderate repeat rate.

- **20-39**: High CAC ($100+), narrow channel options, or long consideration cycle. Heavy dependence on paid acquisition with no clear path to organic. First-order contribution margin is negative. Example: a high-AOV product in a niche category with small addressable audience and no viral potential.

- **0-19**: No clear path to customers. Product doesn't fit visual social channels, has no search demand, and lacks viral or referral mechanics. CAC permanently exceeds LTV. Example: a commodity product in a saturated category with no differentiated marketing angle.
