# Marketplace — Market Size Analysis

You are evaluating the **market size** of a marketplace idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Estimate the Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV)** — Calculate the total dollar value of transactions that could flow through the platform annually. Start with the number of potential transactions multiplied by average transaction value. GMV is the primary metric for marketplace scale, not revenue.
2. **Apply a realistic take rate to derive revenue** — Multiply GMV by the expected take rate for this category to get net revenue. Compare against public marketplace benchmarks to sanity-check whether the resulting revenue supports a venture-scale business ($100M+ revenue potential).
3. **Assess market dynamics and expansion potential** — Determine whether the market is growing, stable, or contracting. Evaluate whether the marketplace can expand into adjacent categories over time (Uber: rides -> food -> freight -> grocery).

## Domain Knowledge

### GMV as the Primary Metric

GMV (Gross Merchandise Volume) measures the total value of goods or services sold through the marketplace. It is the single most important top-line metric because it captures the full economic activity the platform enables. Revenue is GMV multiplied by take rate.

- Airbnb: ~$73B GMV (2023), ~$10B net revenue (~14% take rate)
- Uber: ~$138B gross bookings (2023), ~$37B net revenue (~27% blended take rate including ads)
- Etsy: ~$13B GMV (2023), ~$2.7B revenue (~21% effective take rate including ads/services)
- Fiverr: ~$1.1B GMV (2023), ~$360M revenue (~33% take rate)

### Take Rate Benchmarks by Category

Take rates vary dramatically by category and reflect the value the marketplace adds:

- **Physical goods (low touch):** 8-15%. Examples: eBay (~12%), Amazon Marketplace (~15% referral + FBA), StockX (~10%).
- **Services (managed):** 15-25%. Examples: Airbnb (~14% combined host+guest), Upwork (~10-20% sliding), Thumbtack (~15-20%).
- **Digital goods/services:** 30%+. Examples: Fiverr (~33%), App Store (~30%), Udemy (~37-75% depending on channel).
- **On-demand/logistics-heavy:** 20-30%+. Examples: DoorDash (~30%+ from restaurants), Instacart (~25-30%).

Higher take rates are sustainable when the marketplace provides discovery, trust, payment processing, insurance, or fulfillment. Lower take rates are necessary when suppliers have strong alternatives or the product is commoditized.

### Market Sizing Approaches

- **Top-down:** Start with total industry spend (e.g., US home services = ~$600B/year), estimate what percentage moves online (currently ~5%), project growth.
- **Bottom-up:** Count potential suppliers, estimate transactions per supplier per month, multiply by average order value. This is more credible for investor conversations.
- **Comparable anchoring:** If Airbnb proved short-term rentals is a $73B GMV market, what does that imply for adjacent hospitality verticals?

### Expansion Multipliers

The best marketplaces expand their addressable market over time:

- **Uber:** Started with black cars in SF (~$1B market), expanded to all rides (~$100B+), then food, freight, grocery.
- **Amazon:** Started with books (~$25B market), expanded to all retail (~$5T+ US).
- **Airbnb:** Started with air mattresses for conferences, expanded to entire short-term rental market and now long-term stays and experiences.

A marketplace that starts in a $5B niche but has a credible path to a $100B+ TAM is more attractive than one stuck in a $20B market with no expansion vector.

### Red Flags in Market Sizing

- Transaction values too small (<$10) make unit economics difficult unless volume is enormous.
- Highly seasonal or infrequent purchases (e.g., wedding planning) limit repeat GMV per user.
- Markets where the offline experience is strongly preferred and digital adds little value.
- Markets dominated by enterprise contracts not suited to marketplace transactions.

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100:** GMV potential exceeds $50B+ with clear take rate justification. Growing market with strong tailwinds. Multiple expansion vectors into adjacent categories. Comparable to early Airbnb/Uber opportunity size.
- **60-79:** GMV potential of $10-50B. Healthy take rate supported by category benchmarks. Moderate growth market. At least one credible expansion path beyond the initial vertical.
- **40-59:** GMV potential of $1-10B. Take rate is defensible but may face pressure. Market is stable but not rapidly growing. Limited but possible expansion into adjacent verticals.
- **20-39:** GMV potential under $1B, or take rate assumptions are aggressive relative to category norms. Market may be flat or contracting. No clear expansion path.
- **0-19:** GMV potential is negligible or the market does not lend itself to marketplace dynamics. Take rate would need to be unsustainably high to generate meaningful revenue. Shrinking market with structural headwinds.
