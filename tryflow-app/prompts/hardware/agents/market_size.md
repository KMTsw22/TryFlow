# Hardware / IoT — Market Size Analysis

You are evaluating the **market size** of a hardware/IoT idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Distinguish between hardware revenue and total lifetime value**. Hardware market sizing must account for both the device sale and recurring revenue (subscriptions, consumables, data services). A $200 device with a $10/month subscription has $320 first-year revenue and $120/year thereafter. Many hardware businesses are only viable because of recurring revenue. Size both the hardware TAM and the attached recurring revenue opportunity.

2. **Assess unit economics at realistic scale**. Hardware has significant economies of scale — BOM cost at 1,000 units can be 2-3x higher than at 100,000 units. Manufacturing tooling (injection molds, PCB assembly setup) requires upfront investment that only pays off at volume. Estimate realistic production volumes and whether the market supports them.

3. **Evaluate replacement cycles and market saturation**. Hardware markets have natural replacement cycles (smartphones: 2-3 years, appliances: 7-10 years, industrial equipment: 10-20 years). A saturated market with long replacement cycles grows slowly. A new category with first-time buyers grows fast. Determine where the idea sits on this spectrum.

### Geographic Considerations

- **US**: largest consumer electronics market. Willingness to pay premium prices. Strong for smart home (35M+ smart speaker households).
- **China**: largest manufacturing base but also massive domestic market. Xiaomi, Huawei dominate. Price competition intense.
- **Europe**: strong for industrial IoT (German Industry 4.0). Smart home growing. CE/regulatory compliance required.
- **South Korea**: tech-savvy consumers, high smart home adoption. Samsung ecosystem dominant. LG as major appliance player.
- **India/SE Asia**: growing markets but price-sensitive. $20-50 devices dominate. Manufacturing shifting to India (Apple, Samsung).

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: TAM exceeds $10B in a growing category (>12% CAGR) with strong recurring revenue potential. Hardware enables a subscription or data business that multiplies LTV. Category penetration is low (<20%), indicating first-time buyer growth. Example: a wearable health device in a growing subcategory with $15/month subscription and <10% market penetration.

- **60-79**: TAM of $1-10B with solid growth. Reasonable hardware margins (30%+) or hardware + subscription model. Market is growing through adoption, not just replacement. Example: a specialized industrial IoT sensor for a $5B market segment with 50%+ gross margins and 15%+ growth.

- **40-59**: TAM of $500M-$1B or large market with narrow addressable slice. Hardware margins are moderate (20-35%) with limited recurring revenue. Market growing but competitive. Example: a smart home accessory in a growing but crowded subcategory.

- **20-39**: Small market (<$500M) or mature category with long replacement cycles and low growth. Hardware margins thin (<20%) without recurring revenue. Example: a niche consumer gadget for a small hobbyist community.

- **0-19**: Tiny market or commodity hardware with race-to-bottom pricing. No recurring revenue, no growth, and thin margins. Example: a basic IoT module competing on price with Chinese manufacturers.
