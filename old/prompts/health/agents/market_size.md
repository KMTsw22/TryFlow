# Health & Wellness — Market Size Analysis

You are evaluating the **market size** of a health/wellness idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Identify the addressable segment** within digital health. The global digital health market exceeds $330B and is growing at 15-20% CAGR, but it spans wildly different sub-markets. Determine whether the idea targets clinical health tech (EHR, telehealth, remote patient monitoring) or consumer wellness (fitness, meditation, nutrition tracking). These segments have fundamentally different unit economics and buyer profiles.

2. **Estimate per-user value and realistic penetration**. Clinical health tech commands $500-5,000/year per user because it replaces or augments expensive clinical workflows (Livongo charged ~$75/PMPM for diabetes management; Teladoc bills $40-75 per virtual visit). Consumer wellness sits at $50-200/year per user (Calm charges ~$70/yr, Peloton app ~$160/yr, Noom ~$200/yr). Map the idea to the correct range, then estimate a realistic user base within the target geography and demographic.

3. **Map the payer landscape and willingness to pay**. Health spending flows through four distinct payers, each with different sales cycles and price sensitivity:
   - **Patients (out-of-pocket)**: Fastest to reach, lowest willingness to pay, highest churn. Works for low-cost wellness apps.
   - **Employers**: Spend $15,000+/employee/year on healthcare; motivated by ROI on reduced claims. Castlight Health, Virgin Pulse, and Wellhub (formerly Gympass) target this channel.
   - **Insurers**: Payers like UnitedHealth, Anthem, and Aetna cover digital therapeutics when clinical evidence exists. Omada Health and Pear Therapeutics (before bankruptcy) pursued this.
   - **Government (Medicare/Medicaid, NHS, NHI)**: Largest total spend but slowest procurement. CMS Remote Therapeutic Monitoring codes (98975-98981) opened new billing pathways in 2022.

### Geographic Considerations

- **US**: ~45% of global digital health spend. Fragmented payer system creates complexity but also willingness to pay for solutions that reduce cost.
- **Europe**: GDPR and MDR create regulatory overhead but single-payer systems can deploy at national scale (NHS app serves 30M+ users).
- **Asia-Pacific**: Fastest growth region. Ping An Good Doctor (China) has 400M+ registered users. Japan and South Korea have aging populations driving demand.
- **South Korea**: National Health Insurance covers 97%+ of the population. KFDA has been increasingly open to digital health approvals. Samsung Health ecosystem provides a platform base.

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100**: TAM exceeds $10B in a fast-growing segment (>15% CAGR). Clear payer willingness demonstrated by existing spend. Multiple revenue streams across payer types. Example: AI-powered chronic disease management platform with employer + insurer channels.

- **60-79**: TAM of $1-10B with solid growth. At least one proven payer channel with established willingness to pay. Per-user economics align with comparable companies. Example: specialized telehealth for a large chronic condition (diabetes, MSK).

- **40-59**: TAM of $500M-$1B or a large market where the idea captures a narrow slice. Payer willingness is plausible but not strongly validated. Growth rate is moderate (8-15%). Example: nutrition coaching app for a specific demographic.

- **20-39**: TAM below $500M or relies on unproven payer willingness. The target population is small or the per-user value is unclear. Comparable companies have struggled to scale revenue. Example: wellness app for a rare condition with no payer reimbursement path.

- **0-19**: Tiny or shrinking market. No clear payer. Per-user value is negligible or the target population is too small to build a venture-scale business. Example: a niche fitness tracker for an activity with a small enthusiast community and no clinical angle.
