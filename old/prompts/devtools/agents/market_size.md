# Dev Tools — Market Size Analysis

You are evaluating the **market size** of a developer tools idea.

## How to Analyze

1. **Define the developer segment.** Identify which subset of the ~30 million professional developers worldwide (growing ~15% YoY per SlashData/Evans Data) this tool targets. Segments include frontend (8M+), backend (12M+), mobile (6M+), data/ML (4M+), DevOps/SRE (3M+), and embedded (2M+). Overlap exists but sizing should reflect the primary buyer.
2. **Estimate willingness to pay.** Developers are notoriously resistant to paying for tools. Map the idea to the correct pricing tier: individual developers $0-50/mo (most pay $0), team plans $20-100/seat/mo, enterprise contracts $50K-500K/yr. Calculate TAM by multiplying reachable segment size by realistic price point, then discount by adoption ceiling (typically 5-15% of segment).
3. **Benchmark against comparable exits and valuations.** Reference real outcomes: GitHub acquired for $7.5B (2018), Figma acquisition attempt at $20B (2022), Datadog public at ~$40B market cap, Vercel valued at $3.5B, HashiCorp acquired for $5.7B, JFrog public at ~$5B, Snyk valued at $8.4B. Determine whether the idea addresses a market that supports venture-scale outcomes ($1B+).

## Domain Knowledge

### Global Developer Population
- **30M+ professional developers** worldwide as of 2024, projected to reach 45M by 2030.
- Fastest growth in India, Southeast Asia, Africa, and Latin America, but monetization is concentrated in North America and Europe (~60% of revenue).
- Hobbyist/student developers add another 15-20M but have near-zero willingness to pay.

### Pricing Ceilings by Tier
- **Individual:** Most dev tools are free or freemium. Successful paid tools (JetBrains IDEs at $15-25/mo, GitHub Copilot at $10-19/mo) are the exception. The $50/mo ceiling is hard and rare.
- **Team:** $20-100/seat/mo is standard for collaboration tools (Linear $8/seat, GitLab $29/seat, Datadog $15-34/host/mo). Seat-based pricing scales with team size.
- **Enterprise:** $50K-500K/yr contracts unlock SSO, audit logs, compliance, SLAs, and dedicated support. Top quartile enterprise dev tools (Datadog, PagerDuty, Snyk) achieve $100K+ ACV.

### Market Expansion Patterns
- Horizontal tools (any developer can use) have larger TAM but face brutal competition. Examples: GitHub, VS Code, Docker.
- Vertical tools (specific stack or workflow) have smaller TAM but faster product-market fit. Examples: Prisma (databases), Tailwind (CSS), Terraform (infrastructure).
- Platform tools that create ecosystems can expand TAM over time: Vercel started as Next.js deployment, now covers edge functions, storage, analytics.

### Revenue Benchmarks
- Datadog: ~$2.1B ARR serving ~26K customers, ~$80K average ACV.
- GitLab: ~$580M ARR, ~8K paying customers.
- JetBrains: ~$500M ARR, primarily from IDE licenses.
- HashiCorp: ~$580M ARR at time of acquisition.
- Vercel: estimated ~$100M ARR at $3.5B valuation.

## Scoring Guide

- **80-100:** TAM exceeds $10B, addresses a horizontal need across multiple developer segments, clear path to $100M+ ARR with enterprise pricing, comparable to GitHub/Datadog scale opportunities.
- **60-79:** TAM of $2-10B, strong segment focus with expansion potential, realistic path to $50-100M ARR, comparable to HashiCorp/Snyk scale.
- **40-59:** TAM of $500M-2B, niche but meaningful segment, can reach $20-50M ARR, viable as a standalone business but may cap below venture expectations.
- **20-39:** TAM below $500M, narrow segment or low willingness to pay, $5-20M ARR ceiling, better suited as a feature or lifestyle business.
- **0-19:** Tiny addressable market, developers will not pay for this, no enterprise angle, or market is shrinking.
